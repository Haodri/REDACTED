ARCHIVE FILE 2026 — DIGITAL FOUNDATIONS MODULE. variables-parameters  This document contains a continuous record of visual experiments, typographic systems, and digital methodologies developed throughout the semester. Each section represents an iteration of exploration in computational drawing, grid construction, parametric thinking, and visual communication. The student investigated the transformation of analogue processes into digital workflows and explored generative systems as visual strategies. Drawing algorithms were used to create structures that simulate organic growth and mechanical precision through code. Grid systems were explored as both structural frameworks and visual languages. The relationship between typography and modular grids was analyzed in terms of rhythm and readability. Digital drawing tools enabled the translation of manual gestures into programmable systems. Reflection and documentation accompanied each experiment and formed the basis of this archive. The exploration of algorithmic drawing revealed relationships between repetition and variation. Parameter-based design enabled a systematic expansion of visual possibilities. Grids & Modules  Further studies investigated modular systems as a foundation for complex compositions. By combining simple geometric rules, layered structures emerged that demonstrate how complexity can arise from minimal constraints. The documentation process itself became part of the design methodology, including annotated diagrams, screenshots, and reflective writing. drawing-algorithms  The archive demonstrates a convergence of digital craft and conceptual thinking. It emphasizes the importance of process over outcome and the value of exploration as a method of knowledge production. Additional experiments focused on visual rhythm, repetition, and fragmentation. By dividing compositions into modular units, complex structures emerged from simple components. The student tested grid-based layouts in combination with expressive typography, examining how rigid systems can coexist with fluid visual gestures. Through these investigations, a personal design language began to form—one that balances order and chaos, precision and imperfection.
